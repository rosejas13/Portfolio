# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, recruiters, and fellow engineers visiting jcrose.dev. They arrive curious about who Jasper is as an engineer and as a person. They want to assess depth (technical range, project quality) and fit (personality, communication, culture) within a single browsing session — often on a first pass of 30–60 seconds.

Secondary audience: clients and collaborators evaluating whether Jasper's craft and working style match their needs.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that communicates both competence and character. It exists to leave a confident, memorable impression — not just "this person can code" but "this person I'd want to work with." Success is a visitor who, after one visit, can describe both Jasper's technical range and his personality.

## Brand Personality

Motivated, patient, fun. Simultaneously goofy and 100% focused. The brand voice is clear and concise — never bloated, never performative. There's a playfulness that surfaces naturally without undercutting the professionalism. Like someone who takes the work seriously but doesn't take themselves too seriously.

## Anti-references

- Generic "dev portfolio" templates (dark backgrounds, green terminal text, overused gradient accents)
- Gaudy or overdecorated designs — busy backgrounds, excessive shadows, gratuitous animations
- Boring white minimal templates that look unfinished or indistinguishable from a starter template
- SaaS-style hero-metric layouts (big number, small label, gradient accent)
- Overly serious, sterile corporate aesthetics that hide personality

## Design Principles

- **Soft base, sharp points.** The foundation is calm, warm, and approachable. Important elements (CTAs, active states, key content) get deliberate contrast and "pop" to guide attention.
- **Clear and concise.** Every element earns its place. No decorative fluff, no redundant copy. The interface respects the visitor's time.
- **Consistent without being monotonous.** A unified system (colors, spacing, type) with moments of deliberate surprise. Rhythm, not repetition.
- **Personality without performance.** The fun shows through craft — a micro-interaction, a warm color choice, a clever layout move — not through explicit decoration.
- **Craft that signals care.** Subtle effects (hover transitions, smooth theme toggle, refined borders) communicate that the same attention to detail applies to the code underneath.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.2 AA minimum contrast
- Supports both light and dark themes via system preference and manual toggle
- Reduced motion respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Respects color blindness — never rely on color alone to convey information
